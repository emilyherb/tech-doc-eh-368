Introduction

Hair is a filamentous biomaterial composed of keratin, primarily produced from hair follicles located in the skin. Its purpose is to help our bodies regulate temperature, and when located elsewhere on the body, filter out irritants. Beyond that, hair can have a psychological impact on us as well.

Hair Anatomy

Hair is a filamentous biomaterial composed of keratin, primarily produced from hair follicles located in the skin. This section provides an overview of the biological process behind hair growth and the anatomical structure of hair.

Understanding the primary components of hair structures is essential for studying hair growth:

  • Hair shaft
  • Follicle
  • Root

Hair Shaft

The visible part of the hair that extends beyond the scalp.

Hair Follicle

A tube-like structure located beneath the skin's surface that anchors the hair. Contains the hair bulb, where hair cells rapidly divide to form the hair strand.

Hair Root

The part of a hair that's located in the skin and extends into deeper layers.

Hair Growth

The hair follicle is the structure responsible for hair production. Hair growth occurs in a cycle consisting of three main stages:

  • Anagen - Growth Phase
  • Catogen - Transition Phase
  • Telogen - Resting Phase

Hair Types

Hair comes in various types, characterized by texture, thickness, and curl pattern. The four main types of hair textures are:

  • Straight
  • Wavy
  • Curly
  • Coily

They can further be defined by density (low, medium, or high) or thickness of each individual strand (fine, medium, or thick).

// Function to determine hair texture type function getHairTexture(type) { if (type === "straight") { return "Hair is smooth, no curl or wave."; } else if (type === "wavy") { return "Hair has a gentle wave pattern."; } else if (type === "curly") { return "Hair forms well-defined curls."; } else if (type === "coily") { return "Hair has a tight zigzag pattern."; } else { return "Invalid hair type."; } }
Hair Loss

Hair loss can be caused by various factors, including:

  • Genetic Factors - Androgenic alopecia, commonly known as male/female pattern baldness, is the most common cause.
  • Stress - Stress can cause telogen effluvium, where hair sheds prematurely.
  • Hormonal Changes - Pregnancy, menopause, and other hormonal changes can lead to temporary hair loss.
  • Nutritional Deficiencies - Lack of key nutrients like iron or biotin can contribute to hair thinning.

Treatments are available over the counter as well as by prescripton depending on the severity.

Scalp and Hair Problems

In addition to hair loss, people commonly experience hair problems that can be addressed with proper care:

  • Dandruff - Can be treated with anti-dandruff shampoos containing ingredients like zinc pyrithione or ketoconazole.
  • Frizz - Use anti-frizz products and avoid excessive heat styling.
  • Split Ends - Regularly trim hair to remove split ends and prevent further damage.
  • Oily Hair - Wash hair frequently and use a clarifying shampoo to remove excess oils.
  • Thinning Hair - Try using volumizing products or consult a dermatologist for further treatment.
